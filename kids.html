Fun Facts

FOR KIDS

Explore fun and simple space facts!

  • Space is completely silent: There’s no air in space, which means sound waves can’t travel through it. Space is eerily quiet!


  • The hottest planet isn’t the closest one to the Sun: Even though Mercury is closest to the Sun, Venus is the hottest planet because of its thick atmosphere that traps heat.


  • One day on Venus is longer than one year on Venus: It takes Venus 243 Earth days to rotate once on its axis but only 225 Earth days to go around the Sun. This means a day on Venus lasts longer than a year!


  • Jupiter has giant storms: The Great Red Spot on Jupiter is a massive storm that’s been raging for more than 300 years. It’s so big that it could fit two Earths inside it!


  • Neutron stars are super dense: A teaspoon of matter from a neutron star would weigh about a billion tons on Earth.


  • There are more stars in the universe than grains of sand on all of Earth’s beaches: The universe is so vast that it contains an unimaginable number of stars—trillions and trillions!


  • Saturn isn’t the only planet with rings: While Saturn is famous for its stunning rings, Jupiter, Uranus, and Neptune also have ring systems, though they’re less visible.


  • A day on Mars is only a little longer than a day on Earth: Mars has a day length of about 24.6 hours, making it quite close to Earth’s 24-hour day.
